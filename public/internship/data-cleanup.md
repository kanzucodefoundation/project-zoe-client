# Project Zoe Internship — Data Clean-up Track

## What it is
Taking ~5 years of fellowship and Sunday attendance data (currently in Google Sheets and PDFs), cleaning it into a standard format, uploading it into Project Zoe, and verifying it's correct. Sprint length: 4-6 weeks.

## Entry bar
- Comfortable in Google Sheets (formulas, filtering, basic cleanup)
- Patient with repetitive work
- Trustworthy with real member/attendance data

## Trial task (this is the real interview)
Within 48 hours:
1. Clean one small sample batch (one fellowship, one year) against the standard template
2. Flag anything ambiguous rather than guessing

## How work is assigned
- Data is split into batches by year + fellowship — never one giant undifferentiated pile
- One standard cleaning template (columns, expected formats, validation rules) so every intern's output is uniform before upload
- **Separation of duties:** whoever cleans a batch does not verify it. A different intern cross-checks the uploaded data against the original source before it's signed off. This isn't optional — it's how errors get caught.

## Data handling rules
This is real member attendance data, so:
- No downloading batches to personal devices
- No sharing data outside the assigned task
- Questions about a record go to your track coordinator, not guesswork

## Who to contact
Your track coordinator for template questions or ambiguous records.

## What "good" looks like at sprint end
Assigned batches cleaned, uploaded, and verified by a second person, with a short note on any data quality issues found in the source material.
