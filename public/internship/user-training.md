# Project Zoe Internship — User Training Track

## What it is
Training church leaders and members to use Project Zoe: submitting fellowship reports, adding fellowship members, and following up on first-time guests. Sprint length: 4-6 weeks. Lowest technical barrier, highest impact — only 7 of 230+ churches are onboarded so far, and this track directly drives that number.

## Entry bar
- Comfortable presenting to non-technical adults
- Patient, clear communicator
- No coding background required

## Trial task (this is the real interview)
Within 48 hours:
1. Walk a peer through submitting a mock fellowship report using the provided script
2. Collect their feedback using the standard feedback form

If you can teach the happy path clearly, you're in.

## How work is assigned
- You'll get a fixed script/deck plus a short screen recording of the happy path (submit report → add member → follow up on a guest)
- Paired with a specific cluster or batch of churches for your sprint — e.g. "get these 3 churches submitting reports weekly," not a vague "help train users"
- After each session, collect feedback with the standard form — this doubles as a free source of UX bugs for the QA/coding tracks

## Who to contact
Your track coordinator for scheduling and which churches you're assigned.

## What "good" looks like at sprint end
Your assigned churches actively submitting reports (not just "trained once"), feedback forms collected from every session, and a handoff note on which churches need follow-up.
